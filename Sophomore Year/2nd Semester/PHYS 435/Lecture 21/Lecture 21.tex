Define 

\begin{equation}
    \vec{B} = \vec{\nabla} \times \vec{A}
\end{equation}

Then 

\begin{equation}
    \vec{\nabla} \times B = -\mu_0 \vec{J}
\end{equation}
When choosing the Coulomb gauge, we obtain:
\begin{equation}
    \implies \nabla^2 A = \mu_0 \vec{J}
\end{equation}

Then 

\begin{equation}
    A = \frac{\mu_0}{4\pi}\int d^3 r' \frac{\vec{J}(r)}{|r - r'|}
\end{equation}
